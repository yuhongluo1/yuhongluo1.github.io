Modern World History · February 2025

Colonialism — Justification or Oppression?

A research essay examining the motivations and impacts of European colonialism in Africa.

"Colonialism is the practice of one country taking full or partial political control of another country and occupying it with settlers to profit from its resources and economy." (Longley) In short, colonialism is exploiting another country's resources and controlling it. Its motivations include "profit by exploiting the human and economic resources of the countries they colonized", which means utilizing, usually without permission of local population, resources for a broader market and trade. (Longley) Although people justify colonialism and imperialism like Cecil Rhodes, colonialism itself is an oppressive act.

King Leopold II of Belgium proposed his opinion upon Belgium's colonization upon Congo in his letter to a Belgian Minister of Foreign Affairs, in which he mentioned they were going to "bring civilization to the savage populations", and for the "good fortune of the whole world". King Leopold II and many other European leaders suggested justified their brutal acts like forced labor and cruel penalties by stating they were trying to civilize and implant European values and religion on African natives. (Anderson)

Nonetheless, there were also revolt voices. Jomo Kenyatta suggested that Europeans arrived Africa with their so called "civilization" and religious, but what they actually did was occupying Africans' lands and resources.

Comparing these conflicting views, the impact that colonialism and imperialism brought was destructive upon Africa's economy and culture. [excerpt]

Modern World History · February 2025

Dear Pere Augustin

A letter from a colonized African student to his French missionary teacher, grappling with Enlightenment ideals and identity under colonial rule.

Dear Pere Augustin,

I want to express my invaluable gratitude to you for offering me the chance to step into the field of knowledge and civilization. Though I have some divergence with my parents about my career, and they are not fully supportive of me of receiving education, I do not regret taking on this journey with you. If I took my father's old path, I would never realize the truth of humanity, justice and Christianity. You unveiled a world that was unreachable for the old me.

From all the things you taught me, I was most fascinated by the Enlightenment. I feel sympathetic of the people at that time, trying to discover new ideas from the old and filthy. I remember you've talked about liberty, and how the successors of the Enlightenment try to integrate concepts of liberty and justice into laws. Paris, as the center of Enlightenment movement in France, has always been my dream place to study law. I'm eager to learn about French laws to arm me with weapons of knowledge and civilization. I want to aid my people with the power of liberation.

However, I'm still aware of the disperse of my identity and yours. I've suffered cold eyes because of my skin color, and rejected by libraries because of my accent. I'm aware that my way of perusing knowledge is rigorous and harsh, but my determination of bringing hope to my homeland won't let these obstacles impede me.

After I'm back from Paris, I want to bring change. The crucial lesson I learned is education is key. Only the spread of education enables the growth of a person, and the growth of a country. Knowledge is key, knowledge is invaluable. And law is the kernel.

I still have a last question for you. Do you believe that under the colonial edifice, someone like me would ever be permitted to entitle same rights as you? And are my ambitions achievable?

Sincerely yours,
Amadou Diallo

AP Physics 1 · June 2025

A Conceptual Wheelchair with Mecanum Wheels & Cushion-Based Shock Absorption

A research paper (co-authored with Bella Zhao) designing a low-cost, agile wheelchair for improved mobility and comfort.

Introduction

Wheelchairs play an important role in enhancing mobility and the quality of life for the disabled. Unfortunately, traditional wheelchairs often face challenges such as difficulty making turns in tight spaces. Also, some lack shock absorption systems — features that are typically only available in expensive, high-end models.

Our idea of designing a cost-efficient and agile wheelchair comes from real-life experience and observation. When pushing a friend with her leg injured in a wheelchair, we found it difficult to turn in narrow spaces. Turning the wheelchair around requires pushing it back and forth multiple times because the rear two wheels are too rigid.

To address these issues, we are planning a project to design a wheelchair that improves operability and comfort while remaining low-cost. By incorporating better turning mechanisms and affordable shock absorbers, this innovation seeks to make wheelchairs more accessible and practical for patients worldwide.

Design Overview

Our wheelchair design uses Mecanum wheels (omnidirectional wheels with rollers at a 45° angle, allowing lateral movement and zero-radius turns), a dual-layer cushion (honeycomb-shaped gel on top, polyurethane on bottom for shock absorption), and a Bluetooth joystick control system.

The four Mecanum wheels (200mm diameter) are each driven by a 12V/30RPM DC gear motor, controlled via an Arduino with Bluetooth. The cushion uses a 38mm honeycomb gel layer for breathability and pressure distribution, and a 38mm 40A polyurethane layer for shock absorption — totalling around 7cm thickness.

Key Innovation

Unlike existing designs that achieve shock absorption through expensive mechanical suspension systems, our design uses cost-effective materials: polyurethane (~$20/sq ft) and honeycomb gel (~$30/pad) — keeping total cost significantly lower than high-end wheelchairs while maintaining comfort and maneuverability.

Advocacy & Reflection

Our wheelchair aims to improve current electric wheelchair designs by innovating on mobility, shock-absorption, comfort, agility, and affordability. Challenges arise in balancing cost-effectiveness with ideal performance. Future iterations could explore lighter materials or simplified controls to further enhance usability and reduce costs.

AP Statistics · November 2025

Analysis of ELA Test Refusal Rates in NY Public Schools

A statistical analysis of Grade 3-8 ELA test refusal rates across 1,011 NY public schools (2023-2024), using histograms, boxplots, and Q-Q plots.

Introduction

In this report, we aim to find out the distribution of percent students that refused to take Grade 3-8 ELA tests in public schools during school year 2023-2024 in New York. In particular, we want to determine if the data listed is normally distributed or not by multiple methods such as shape of histogram and Q-Q plot.

Methodology

We analyzed refusal rates across 1,011 public schools (population mean: 9.65%, median: 5.5%, standard deviation: 11.6%). Tools used: Microsoft Excel and R for data analysis and graphing.

Findings

Histogram: A strongly right-skewed distribution was observed — the vast majority of schools have refusal rates clustered between 0% and 20%, while a few show very high rates. This clearly deviates from a normal bell-shaped curve.

Boxplot: Multiple large outliers were detected. The interval between Q1 and median is much shorter than between Q2 and median, confirming a right-skewed distribution.

Q-Q Plot: Data points curve upward away from the reference line in the upper right section, a classic indicator of right-skewed distribution — the largest refusal rates are more extreme than expected under normality.

Conclusion

Our results reject the hypothesis that ELA test refusal rates are normally distributed. The vast majority of NY public schools took the ELA test in 2023-2024, making refusal a rare phenomenon. However, a minority of schools still show high refusal rates — the underlying reasons (societal, economic, or policy-related) could be further investigated.

— Co-authored with Maxime Shen, Nick Yang & Bella Zhao. Full paper with citations available on request.

Modern World History · November 2024

Why the Industrial Revolution Began in Britain

An essay examining the origins of the Industrial Revolution in Britain, its global spread, and its positive and negative effects on society.

The Industrial Revolution is a crucial series of historical events that originated in Britain in the 1800s and soon spread all globally. The reasons why Industrial Revolution began in Britain, its process of spreading globally and the effects on society will be following discussed.

The country of Britain has been widely accepted as the birthplace of the Industrial Revolution due to the boost in productivity caused by economic growth. Locally, Britain is rich in coal reserves, while coal was the main fuel and power source for all kinds of machineries at that time. Also, ideas from the Enlightenment developed to a more mature stage that can be applicated to the society. Another reason is the British system — the government encouraged trading and free economic activities, and the protection of private properties.

As a counterexample, things similar to the Industrial Revolution didn't occur in China due to the restrictions of the government. Natural resources like coal were less accessible because of wide distribution and difficulty in mining. Feudalism was still dominant; people's minds were still concrete and locked in feudalism. Strict regulations proclaimed by the Chinese government restricted people from innovating.

Without the Industrial Revolution, we won't be living our lives like this now. The significant growth of productivity due to mechanization leaded to urbanization, which constructs modern "cities". However, everything is double-sided. The Industrial Revolution also leaded to damage on the society — industrially advanced countries exploited under-development countries and slaves to provide them the resource and cheap labor, which intensified inequality in the world.

2022年的五月份

一段不太好的日子。写于初二。/ A difficult time, written in 8th grade.

2022年的五月份，在经历了小中考一系列的压力后，我变得非常厌恶学习，甚至是恐惧学习，恐惧上学，恐惧和学校有关的一切。我不知道我是怎么了，我觉得我病了，但是我又觉得我是在自作多情。那段时间，我经常请假，经常不去学校。最严重的时候，我害怕与同学交流，我害怕看到卷子，害怕看到作业，甚至害怕老师讲课时的眼神。

我记得一开始症状还没那么严重的时候，我很焦虑。焦虑成绩、排名这些事情已经是表面的了，我焦虑的是我不学习。我一旦不学习，一旦不把时间浪费在书桌前，我就会本能的焦虑，像有人在不断的告诉我你必须学习，你必须稳住自己。初一下学期，我考出了年级的一的好成绩。但是初二我并没有很好的延续我的优势，甚至连年级前15都没办法进。我越来越着急，也越来越焦虑。我坐不住，一在书桌前坐久我就止不住的想我的成绩，我的排名。每每看到同学比我学习更努力时，我都会崩溃。有时候我崩溃的原因真的很奇怪，像写作业的时候铅芯突然断掉。我经常偷偷哭，我不敢让爸爸妈妈看到我的难过，我想做一个坚强的人。我很怀念过去。

后来，我的情况并没有好转，反倒我觉得自己真的开心不起来了。那段时间，我基本一周只去学校三天。我一个人在家的时候会对着镜子微笑，但是我发现真的笑不出来。我无论如何努力都挤不出一个笑容。我会莫名其妙地哭，可能上一秒还在和同学很开心的聊天，下一秒眼泪就会留下来。过了一段时间，我感觉我已经麻木了。我甚至感觉不到自己在哭。崩溃的时候，我感觉我变成了一个柔软的球，周围什么都没有，这么多年长大构筑的所有都没了，我想回到小时候。情绪稍微好些的时候，我又觉得我像一个长满尖刺的球。任何事情都可能让我变得特别暴躁。那时，我做出迈进学校的决定都无比困难。一整天我的手都止不住的抖，我觉得呼吸很累很累。我可能是胆子太小，我那段时间真的很想死。可能我的主观意识不想，但是感觉我整个身体都在向我反馈想要逃离现在的环境。后来我尝试去了一次学校。我真的没办法控制自己的呼吸，坐在座位上我就会无端的紧张，恶心，难过。

后来我去了医院，做了治疗。

现在我已经好很多了。

5月-7月是我这辈子永远都没办法忘记的一段日子。我每天都在挣扎，我想要变好，我想要痊愈，我讨厌自己现在的状态，我想要回到那一个每天都很开心的日子。那段时间我一直在玩，我完全没有学习，我想要去忘记过去的焦虑。我让自己不要想起关于死亡的事情。我想做一个天天开心的人。我本来一天会发3 4条朋友圈，但是那段时间我连着一个多月什么都没有做。我很想念我的同学和校园生活，但我也惧怕回到自己熟悉的那个环境。
